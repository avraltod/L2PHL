The national mood held steady, until the Middle East crisis

Life satisfaction is the panel's broadest measure of how households feel. It held near OLD out of five through the first five rounds. Then it slipped, to OLD by Round 8. The decline is small, and it lines up with the rounds when the Middle East crisis dominated the news.

The same turn shows in how households judged their own finances. The share saying their economic situation had worsened sat in the teens for months, near OLD, then jumped to OLD at Round 6 before easing to OLD. The mood did not drift, it reacted, because the price of fuel and food moved at the same time.

The mood did not drift. It reacted.

The dip was not confined to one group. Switch the chart to income quintile and every line bends down together around Round 6. A shock that arrives through prices reaches the mood of richer and poorer households alike, because they buy in the same market.
